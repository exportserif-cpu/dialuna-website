DiaLuna (How It Began)

The Night of the Crimson Moon

"Some secrets are meant to stay with the earth. They're never written down. The soil remembers them..."

Crimson Moon Night

"An old legend still told in Sındırgı says..."

Some secrets are meant to stay with the earth.
They're never written down.
The soil remembers them.

People say that only a few nights each year, the moon rises differently.
Its glow drifts across the fields like a crimson veil.
And on that night...
even the wind seems to slow down.

The old farmers call it The Night of the Crimson Moon.
Because they believe that, for one quiet moment, nature holds its breath...
and the moon leaves the earth with a single gift:
Flavor.

🌒

Daughter of the Earth

Centuries ago, in the fertile plains of Sındırgı, Balıkesir, there lived a young woman.
Some called her a farmer.
Some said she was a healer.
Others simply knew her as...
The Daughter of the Earth.
She understood the language of the land.
She could smell the coming season in the rain,
read abundance in the direction of the wind,
and tell the time of year by the warmth of the sun.
One summer, as the whole village rushed to harvest, she waited.
"What are you waiting for?" they asked.
She looked up at the sky.
Then she smiled and said,
"The moon hasn't sealed the tomatoes' secret just yet."

🌕

The Crimson Full Moon

That night, the full moon rose wearing a crimson crown.
The earth cooled.
The wind fell silent.
By sunrise, everyone tasted the same truth.
The tomatoes were brighter.
The peppers were richer.
And the flavor...
was unforgettable.

The Daughter of the Earth simply smiled.
Then she said,
"The sun gives it strength. The moon locks in the flavor."

From that day on, the harvest no longer happened under the blazing sun.
It began in the cool of the night.

The years passed.
Kingdoms rose.
Kingdoms fell.
Generations came and went.
But in Sındırgı, those words were never forgotten.

🌙

DIALUNA Today

Today, science can explain why that tradition works.
We simply know this:
Nature doesn't give its greatest gifts to those who rush.
It rewards those who know how to wait.
That wisdom has been passed down from one generation to the next.
Today, that legacy lives on as DiaLuna.
We harvest our tomatoes in the cool of the night.
We produce them in small, limited batches.
Because real flavor...
isn't rushed.
It's earned through patience.

They say...
On the nights of the full moon, some farmers pause before the harvest.
They don't pray.
They don't make a wish.
They simply wait.
Because they know...
The sun grows the fruit.
The moon preserves its flavor.

Maybe the Night of the Crimson Moon is nothing more than a legend.
Or maybe it still lives on in the fields of Sındırgı.
